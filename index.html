Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about BrainLink 2025

Who can participate in BrainLink? +

BrainLink is open to university students from business and engineering backgrounds. Teams must consist of 3-5 members with at least one business student and one engineering student.

What are the competition phases? +

The competition has two main phases: Semifinals (initial proposal submission) and Finals (developed solution presentation). Top teams from the semifinals advance to the finals.

Is there a registration fee? +

No, participation in BrainLink is completely free for all student teams. The competition is sponsored by our industry partners.

What kind of challenges will we work on? +

Challenges are provided by our partner companies and cover real-world business problems that require both technical and business solutions. Past challenges have included sustainability initiatives, digital transformation, and product innovation.

What prizes can winners receive? +

Prizes include cash awards, internship opportunities with partner companies, mentorship sessions with industry leaders, and recognition at the awards ceremony.
